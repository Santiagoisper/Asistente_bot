## 1. Purpose

This Study Procedures Manual provides operational guidance for the conduct of
the MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 study. It covers site procedures related to
monitoring visit preparation, source documentation expectations, query
resolution, and study closeout.

This document is intended to support site personnel in maintaining the
operational integrity of the study, ensuring that source documents are complete
and accessible, and that monitoring visits are conducted efficiently.

This document contains no real study data, no PHI, and no real sponsor
information. All examples are fictional and used solely for the Ichtys smoke
test.

---

## 2. Scope

This manual applies to:
- Clinical Research Coordinators (CRC) and Site Managers
- Principal Investigators and Sub-Investigators
- Clinical Research Associates (CRA) and Monitors conducting on-site or
  remote monitoring visits
- Study staff responsible for source documentation, eCRF completion, and
  query resolution

All personnel must be trained on this manual and the training documented
in the delegation and training log before participating in any procedure
covered by this manual.

## 3. Procedures / Criteria

### 3.1 Monitoring Visit Preparation

Before each on-site monitoring visit, the CRC and Site Manager must ensure
that all of the following documents and records are complete, organized, and
available for the monitor's review. The documents listed below constitute the
minimum set required for standard monitoring visits during the
MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 study.

**Required documents for monitoring visit:**

1. **Signed Informed Consent Forms (ICF):**
   - Original signed ICF for each enrolled subject
   - Any subsequent re-consent forms if the protocol or IB was updated
   - ICF log showing date of consent, version signed, and subject ID

2. **Source Documents:**
   - Subject medical charts (original or certified copies) covering the study
     period, including pre-existing conditions at screening
   - Visit notes and clinical findings for each study visit
   - Vital signs records (blood pressure, heart rate, weight, temperature)
   - Laboratory results (local and central lab reports)
   - ECG records (at minimum from V1 and V6)
   - Concomitant medication records with start/stop dates and doses

3. **Investigational Product Accountability:**
   - Investigational Product Accountability Log (IPAL) from pharmacy
   - Subject diary cards (returned by subjects at each visit)
   - Dispensing records and return receipts
   - Temperature monitoring logs for the pharmacy refrigerator

4. **Delegation and Training Log:**
   - Current and signed delegation log listing all study personnel, their
     roles, and the dates they were delegated responsibilities by the PI
   - Training certificates or records for each delegated staff member for
     all mandatory trainings (GCP, protocol, Lab Manual, Pharmacy Manual)

5. **Adverse Event and SAE Documentation:**
   - Source records for all AEs documented at each visit
   - Completed SAE Report Forms for any SAEs
   - Follow-up SAE reports if applicable
   - Confirmation of sponsor notification within required timelines

6. **Protocol Deviation Log:**
   - Documented record of any protocol deviations, including:
     - Description of the deviation
     - Subject ID affected
     - Date of occurrence
     - CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action) if required
     - Sponsor notification date and confirmation

7. **eCRF Status:**
   - All eCRF entries up to date for completed visits
   - No unanswered critical queries older than 5 business days
   - Any locked visits reviewed for completeness

8. **Lab Sample Logs:**
   - PK sample processing and shipment logs (see Lab Manual Section 3.4 and 3.5)
   - Chain of custody records for shipped samples
   - Central laboratory receipt confirmations

9. **Query Resolution Status:**
   - List of open queries with dates opened and responsible staff
   - Documentation of resolved queries with explanation and date of resolution

10. **Regulatory File (if applicable):**
    - Current IRB/EC approval including any amendments
    - Current protocol version approved by IRB/EC
    - Investigator's CV and medical license (current)

**Pre-monitoring visit checklist:**
The CRC must complete the Site Pre-Monitoring Checklist (provided in the site
binder) at least 2 business days before the scheduled monitoring visit and
send it to the CRA. The checklist identifies any outstanding items so the
monitor can plan the visit accordingly.

### 3.2 Source Documentation Expectations

Source documents are the original records from which eCRF data are transcribed.
They must be:

- **Contemporaneous:** Recorded at or as close as possible to the time the
  event or procedure occurred, not reconstructed after the fact.
- **Original or certified copies:** Photocopies must be certified by the PI or
  their delegate.
- **Legible:** Entries must be clearly readable. Corrections must use a
  single line through the error, with the date, time, and initials of the
  correcting person. No overwriting or correction fluid.
- **Attributable:** Each entry must be clearly linked to the subject (by
  study ID, not by name or date of birth in study records accessible to
  monitors) and to the person who made the entry.
- **Consistent with the eCRF:** Any discrepancy between source and eCRF must
  be resolved through a query in the eCRF and documented.

The following records are classified as source documents for
MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1:

- Visit notes and assessments
- Lab results (local and central)
- Vital signs
- Diary cards
- IPAL
- SAE reports
- ICF logs
- Sample processing logs (Lab Manual Section 3.5)
- Deviations log

### 3.3 Query Resolution

Queries are raised in the eCRF when data are missing, inconsistent, or
require clarification. All queries must be resolved promptly.

**Query resolution timeline:**
- Critical queries (those related to subject safety, eligibility, or SAEs):
  resolve within 2 business days of receipt.
- Non-critical queries: resolve within 5 business days of receipt.

**Query resolution process:**
1. The CRC or designated staff member reviews the query.
2. The correct response is identified by reviewing source documents.
3. The response is entered in the eCRF with a clear explanation.
4. If the source document must be corrected, the correction is made in the
   source document following the rules in Section 3.2, and the corrected
   source document is reviewed by the monitor at the next monitoring visit.

Open queries outstanding beyond the resolution timeline must be escalated to
the Site Manager and reported in the Pre-Monitoring Checklist.

---

### 3.4 Closeout Readiness

At study completion, the site must prepare for the closeout monitoring visit.
Closeout requires all of the following to be complete before scheduling:

1. All subjects have completed the End of Study visit (V6) or been
   discontinued and have a documented reason for discontinuation.
2. All eCRF entries are complete and no outstanding critical queries.
3. All IP has been returned to pharmacy and the IPAL is reconciled.
4. All source documents are organized and filed in the site binder.
5. All SAEs are followed to resolution and final SAE reports are submitted.
6. Protocol deviation log is complete and any required CAPAs are documented
   and closed.
7. All lab sample logs confirm receipt at the central laboratory.

## 4. Exceptions

### 4.1 Outstanding Query at Monitoring Visit

If critical queries cannot be resolved before a monitoring visit, the CRC must:
1. Notify the monitor in advance via email or phone.
2. Identify the reason for delay and a target resolution date.
3. Ensure the query is escalated if resolution requires medical judgment from
   the PI.

### 4.2 Remote Monitoring

If a monitoring visit is conducted remotely instead of on-site:
1. Source documents must be provided as certified electronic copies via a
   secure document sharing platform designated by the sponsor.
2. The monitor will conduct the verification against eCRF data remotely.
3. Any original documents that cannot be provided electronically (e.g.,
   original signed ICF) must be reviewed at the next on-site visit.
4. Remote monitoring does not replace on-site monitoring; at least one on-site
   visit per subject per study phase is required unless the sponsor grants
   a specific exception.

### 4.3 Protocol Deviation Without CAPA

If a protocol deviation is identified that does not require a CAPA (minor
deviation at the investigator's judgment), the Investigator must still document
the deviation in the deviation log and notify the sponsor within 5 business days.
The sponsor will confirm in writing whether a CAPA is required.

### 4.4 Monitoring Visit Cancelled by Sponsor

If the sponsor cancels or reschedules a monitoring visit, the CRC must:
1. Document the cancellation in the monitoring visit log.
2. Maintain all prepared documents in their organized state until the visit
   is rescheduled.
3. Confirm a new monitoring visit date within 10 business days.
