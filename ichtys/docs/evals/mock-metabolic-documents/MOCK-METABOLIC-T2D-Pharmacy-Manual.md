## 1. Purpose

This Pharmacy Manual describes the procedures for the receipt, storage,
preparation, dispensing, accountability, and return of the investigational
product (IP) IMT-201 used in the MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 clinical study.

It is intended to ensure that IMT-201 is handled correctly at the investigative
site, in compliance with Good Clinical Practice (GCP) and applicable regulations.

This document contains no real pharmaceutical data, no real sponsor product
information, and no proprietary formulation details. All examples are fictional
and used solely for the Ichtys smoke test.

## 2. Scope

This manual applies to:
- Site Pharmacists and Pharmacy Technicians responsible for IP management
- Principal Investigators and Sub-Investigators who authorize dispensing
- Clinical Research Coordinators who support IP accountability documentation

Personnel handling IMT-201 must complete training on this manual before
performing any IP-related activity. Training must be documented in the site's
delegation and training log.

## 3. Procedures / Criteria

### 3.1 Investigational Product Description

**Product name:** IMT-201 (fictional)
**Formulation:** Oral tablet, 50 mg (mock)
**Appearance:** White, round, film-coated tablet with no identifying markings
  (mock description)
**Packaging:** Blister packs of 14 tablets per card, 2 cards per kit
**Kit contents:** 28 tablets per kit (4-week supply at one tablet per day)
**Dosing schedule:** One tablet orally once daily, at any time of day, with
  or without food

IMT-201 tablets must not be split, crushed, or chewed. Subjects must swallow
tablets whole with a glass of water.

---

### 3.2 Storage Conditions

IMT-201 must be stored under the following conditions at all times:

- **Temperature:** 2°C to 8°C (refrigerated)
- **Protection from light:** Yes — store in the original blister packaging
  inside the provided opaque outer carton
- **Humidity:** No special requirements beyond maintaining standard pharmacy
  refrigerator conditions
- **Segregation:** IMT-201 must be stored in a locked, restricted-access
  pharmacy refrigerator designated for investigational products only

Temperature must be monitored continuously using a calibrated temperature
logger. Temperature records must be reviewed weekly and deviations documented
immediately. The sponsor's clinical operations team must be notified of any
temperature excursion (temperature outside 2°C–8°C for more than 4 hours)
within 24 hours.

Expired or damaged IP must be quarantined separately and returned to the
sponsor per Section 3.5.

---

### 3.3 Dispensing

**Authorized dispensing personnel:**
Only the site pharmacist, or a pharmacy technician under direct pharmacist
supervision, may dispense IMT-201. Dispensing by non-pharmacy personnel is
not permitted without specific written authorization from the PI and sponsor.

**Dispensing procedure:**

1. Verify that the subject is enrolled in the study and that the visit is
   recorded in the eCRF.
2. Confirm the subject's study ID and visit number before retrieving kits
   from storage.
3. Retrieve the appropriate number of kits for the visit duration:
   - Visits 1 (first dose at clinic), 2, 3: dispense 1 kit (28 tablets /
     4-week supply)
   - Visit 4: dispense 2 kits (8-week supply through end of treatment)
4. Record the kit number(s), lot number(s), and expiry date(s) in the
   Investigational Product Accountability Log (IPAL).
5. Record the dispensing date, subject ID, visit number, quantity dispensed,
   and the name of the dispensing pharmacist in the IPAL.
6. Provide the subject with a 4-week subject diary card and instructions on
   dose recording.
7. Instruct the subject to:
   - Take one tablet daily
   - Record each dose taken in the diary card
   - Return all unused tablets and empty blister packs at the next visit
   - Contact the site immediately if they miss more than 2 doses in a row

### 3.4 Missed Dose Management

The following rules apply when a subject misses a dose of IMT-201:

**Single missed dose:**
- If the subject realizes they missed a dose and it has been less than
  12 hours since the scheduled dose time, they should take the missed dose
  as soon as possible.
- If more than 12 hours have passed since the scheduled dose time, the
  subject must skip the missed dose entirely and take the next scheduled
  dose at the usual time.
- Under no circumstances should the subject double the dose to compensate
  for a missed one.

**Two or more consecutive missed doses:**
- If a subject misses two or more doses in a row, they must contact the
  site immediately.
- The Investigator will assess the subject's status, review the subject
  diary, and determine whether it is safe for the subject to continue in
  the study.
- The Investigator's assessment and decision must be documented in the
  source record and eCRF.
- If the Investigator determines that the subject can continue, dispensing
  resumes at the next scheduled visit following standard procedure.

**Accountability of missed doses:**
- Unused tablets corresponding to missed doses must be returned at the
  next visit and reconciled in the IPAL.
- Do not re-dispense returned tablets.

See also Protocol Section 3.8 for the protocol-level compliance thresholds
related to missed doses.

### 3.5 Accountability and Returns

**Ongoing accountability:**
The pharmacist must maintain the IPAL throughout the study, recording for
each subject:
- All kits received from the sponsor
- All kits dispensed to subjects (date, visit, quantity)
- All kits returned by subjects (date, quantity, condition)
- All kits destroyed or returned to sponsor

**Subject returns:**
At each visit, the CRC or pharmacist must:
1. Collect all remaining tablets and blister packs from the previous supply
2. Count the returned tablets and record the actual count in the IPAL
3. Calculate: tablets dispensed minus tablets returned minus subject diary
   entries = compliance rate
4. Flag any discrepancies for investigator review

**Returns to sponsor:**
Unused, expired, or damaged IP must be returned to the sponsor at study
completion, or at the sponsor's instruction during the study. Destruction at
site is not permitted without explicit written sponsor approval.

Return shipments must use sponsor-approved packaging and be accompanied by a
completed Return Manifest.

## 4. Exceptions

### 4.1 Temperature Excursion

If the pharmacy refrigerator temperature falls outside the 2°C–8°C range:
1. Document the excursion (start time, temperature observed, duration) in the
   temperature log immediately.
2. Transfer IP to an alternative qualified refrigerator if available.
3. Notify the sponsor's clinical operations team within 24 hours.
4. Do not dispense affected kits until the sponsor confirms they are suitable
   for use.
5. Quarantine affected kits with a clear label indicating they are on hold
   pending sponsor review.

### 4.2 Damaged or Incomplete Kit

If a kit is received damaged or with a broken blister pack:
1. Quarantine the affected kit.
2. Document the condition with photographs if possible.
3. Notify the sponsor's clinical supply team within 24 hours.
4. Do not dispense a damaged kit to any subject.

### 4.3 Consecutive Missed Doses

See Section 3.4. If a subject reports more than two consecutive missed doses
and the Investigator determines that the subject cannot continue safely, the
subject may be discontinued from the study. All remaining IP must be collected
and returned to pharmacy, counted, and reconciled in the IPAL.

### 4.4 Dispensing Error

If a dispensing error is identified (wrong kit number, wrong subject, wrong
quantity):
1. Document the error in the IPAL and the deviation log immediately.
2. Notify the Investigator and the sponsor's clinical operations team within
   24 hours.
3. Assess the impact on subject safety and study integrity per the
   Study Procedures Manual.
