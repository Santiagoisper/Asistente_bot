## 1. Purpose

This Laboratory Manual provides instructions for the collection, processing,
storage, and shipment of biological samples collected in the
MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 clinical study. It ensures that all samples are handled
in a standardized manner to maintain sample integrity and support reliable
pharmacokinetic (PK) and biomarker analyses.

This document contains no real patient data, real laboratory identifiers, real
courier information, or real central laboratory contact details. All names,
codes, and identifiers used in examples are fictional and for smoke testing
purposes only.

Personnel responsible for sample handling must read this manual in full before
performing any sample collection or processing procedure for this study.

## 3. Procedures / Criteria

### 3.1 PK Sample Collection

PK samples are collected at Visits 3 (Week 8) and 4 (Week 12) per the Protocol
Schedule of Assessments.

**Timing:**
- Samples must be collected as a pre-dose (trough) sample within 30 minutes
  before the subject takes their scheduled daily dose of IMT-201.
- The exact collection time (date, hour, and minute) must be recorded in the
  source document and eCRF.

**Collection tube:**
- Use a 4 mL K2-EDTA (purple/lavender top) tube.
- Label the tube immediately after collection with the subject's study ID,
  visit number, collection date, and collection time. Do not use initials or
  names that could identify the subject.

**Volume:**
- Collect a minimum of 3 mL whole blood per PK sample.
- If the volume is insufficient, document the actual volume collected and
  notify the sponsor's central laboratory coordinator.

**Labeling example (mock):**
- Subject ID: MOCK-001
- Visit: V4
- Collection date: 2026-03-15
- Collection time: 08:42

### 3.2 PK Sample Processing

Samples must be processed within 30 minutes of collection. Do not leave
collected samples at room temperature for longer than 30 minutes.

**Processing steps:**

1. After blood collection, gently invert the EDTA tube 8 to 10 times to
   mix with anticoagulant. Do not shake vigorously.
2. Centrifuge at 1500 x g for 10 minutes at 4°C (or as close to 4°C as
   site equipment allows, but no warmer than room temperature).
3. After centrifugation, carefully pipette the plasma (upper layer) into
   labeled cryovials. Avoid disturbing the buffy coat or red blood cell
   layer.
4. Aliquot plasma into two cryovials:
   - Cryovial A (primary): minimum 0.8 mL
   - Cryovial B (backup): remaining plasma, minimum 0.5 mL
5. Label each cryovial with the same information as the collection tube,
   plus an aliquot identifier (A or B).
6. Proceed immediately to storage as described in Section 3.3.

**Documenting processing:**
Record the centrifugation start time, completion time, and any deviations
(e.g., delayed processing, temperature excursion) in the sample processing
log.

---

### 3.3 PK Sample Storage

Processed plasma aliquots must be stored immediately after processing.

- **Required storage temperature:** -60°C or colder (target: -70°C or below).
- Use a site freezer or ultra-low temperature (ULT) freezer designated for
  study samples.
- Samples must not be stored with non-study materials that could cause
  contamination or temperature fluctuations.
- Do not thaw samples unless instructed by the central laboratory.
- Record the freezer temperature daily in the temperature log. If temperature
  exceeds -60°C, notify the sponsor's laboratory contact within 4 hours.

---

### 3.4 PK Sample Shipping

Samples are shipped to the central laboratory on a monthly basis or as
instructed by the sponsor's laboratory coordinator.

**Shipping requirements:**

1. Prepare samples for shipment using the kit provided by the sponsor.
   Do not use unapproved packaging materials.
2. Samples must be shipped on dry ice (solid CO2). The minimum amount of
   dry ice is 5 kg per shipment to ensure maintenance of frozen conditions
   during transit.
3. Complete the Sample Shipment Manifest (provided in the site binder) for
   each shipment, listing all cryovials by subject ID, visit, aliquot type,
   and collection date/time.
4. Contact the sponsor's logistics coordinator at least 48 hours in advance
   to arrange courier pickup. Courier name and contact information are
   provided in the site binder and are not reproduced here.
5. Do not ship samples on Fridays or on the day before a public holiday
   unless the sponsor specifically authorizes an exception.
6. Retain a copy of the signed shipment manifest at the site.

**Chain of custody:**
- Every sample transfer (site to courier, courier to central lab) must be
  documented using the Sample Chain of Custody form.
- Site CRC must confirm receipt notification from the central laboratory
  within 3 business days of shipment. If confirmation is not received,
  notify the sponsor's laboratory coordinator immediately.

### 3.5 Sample Labeling and Chain of Custody

Each cryovial must be labeled before freezing using a waterproof label that
can withstand -70°C storage conditions.

**Required label information:**

- Study ID: MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1
- Subject ID (assigned study number, not name or date of birth)
- Visit number and visit code (e.g., V4 / Week 12)
- Collection date (YYYY-MM-DD format)
- Collection time (HH:MM, 24-hour format)
- Aliquot identifier (A or B)
- Sample type: PK-PLASMA

**Chain of custody log:**
The site must maintain a Sample Chain of Custody log that records each time
a sample changes hands, including:
- Date and time of transfer
- Name and role of person transferring
- Name and role of person receiving
- Reason for transfer (e.g., centrifugation, freezer storage, shipment)

The chain of custody log is a source document and must be available for
monitoring review.

## 4. Exceptions

### 4.1 Hemolyzed Sample

If a sample appears hemolyzed (pink or red plasma after centrifugation):
1. Document the observation in the processing log.
2. Complete processing as described in Section 3.2.
3. Label the cryovials as hemolyzed and notify the sponsor's laboratory
   coordinator immediately.
4. Do not discard the sample unless instructed by the sponsor.
5. Attempt a repeat collection at the next available opportunity only if
   the sample is deemed unusable by the central laboratory.

### 4.2 Delayed Processing

If centrifugation cannot be completed within 30 minutes of collection:
1. Document the delay and reason in the processing log.
2. Notify the sponsor's laboratory coordinator within 24 hours.
3. The central laboratory will evaluate whether the sample can be used.

### 4.3 Temperature Excursion

If the site freezer temperature rises above -60°C for more than 2 hours:
1. Notify the sponsor's laboratory coordinator within 4 hours.
2. Attempt to transfer samples to an alternative freezer.
3. Document the excursion in the freezer temperature log with start and end
   temperatures and duration.
4. Do not ship affected samples until authorization is received from the
   central laboratory.

### 4.4 Shipping Delay

If a scheduled shipment is delayed beyond 1 business day from the planned
date:
1. Notify the sponsor's logistics coordinator immediately.
2. Verify that the freezer temperature remains stable.
3. Reschedule shipment as soon as possible.
4. Document the delay in the site file.
