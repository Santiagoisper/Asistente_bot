## 1. Purpose

This protocol describes the objectives, design, methodology, and procedures for
the MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 study, a Phase 2b clinical trial evaluating IMT-201
in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) and overweight or obesity.

IMT-201 is a fictional investigational agent developed for the purposes of the
Ichtys smoke test. This document does not represent any real clinical study,
sponsor, or investigational product.

This document is intended to provide realistic content density for Ichtys
document ingestion, retrieval, and answer generation testing.

## 3. Procedures / Criteria

### 3.1 Inclusion Criteria

A subject must meet ALL of the following criteria to be eligible for enrollment:

1. Male or female, aged 30 to 75 years inclusive at the time of informed consent.
2. Clinical diagnosis of type 2 diabetes mellitus for at least 12 months prior
   to screening.
3. Glycated hemoglobin (HbA1c) >= 7.0% and <= 10.0% at the Screening Visit (V1),
   as measured by the central laboratory.
4. Body Mass Index (BMI) >= 25 kg/m2 and <= 45 kg/m2 at the Screening Visit.
5. On a stable dose of metformin (1000 mg/day or higher) for at least 8 weeks
   prior to screening, or on diet and exercise only without antidiabetic
   pharmacotherapy.
6. Willing and able to provide written informed consent.
7. Able to comply with study procedures and visit schedule.

Note: A subject with HbA1c of 9.0% meets criterion 3, provided all other
inclusion criteria are also satisfied.

---

### 3.2 Exclusion Criteria

A subject must NOT meet any of the following criteria to be eligible for
enrollment:

1. History of any pancreatitis (acute or chronic) at any time prior to
   enrollment. Subjects with a prior diagnosis of pancreatitis are excluded
   regardless of time since resolution.
2. Type 1 diabetes mellitus or secondary diabetes (e.g., due to pancreatic
   disease, glucocorticoids, or other medications).
3. Estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) < 45 mL/min/1.73m2 at screening.
4. Clinically significant hepatic impairment or alanine aminotransferase (ALT)
   or aspartate aminotransferase (AST) > 3x the upper limit of normal (ULN) at
   screening.
5. History of active malignancy within 5 years prior to screening, except for
   adequately treated basal cell or squamous cell carcinoma of the skin.
6. Pregnancy, breastfeeding, or intention to become pregnant during the study.
7. Use of any prohibited antidiabetic medications within 12 weeks of screening
   (see Section 3.6).
8. Participation in another investigational study within 30 days or 5 half-lives
   (whichever is longer) prior to screening.
9. Any condition that, in the opinion of the Investigator, would prevent the
   subject from safely completing the study or interfere with study objectives.

### 3.4 Visit Windows

All visit windows are relative to the target date calculated from the date of
first dose administration at Visit 1.

| Visit | Target Day | Allowed Window |
|---|---|---|
| V1 (Screening/Baseline) | Day 1 | No window (fixed) |
| V2 (Week 4) | Day 29 | ± 3 days |
| V3 (Week 8) | Day 57 | ± 3 days |
| V4 (Week 12) | Day 85 | ± 3 days |
| V5 (Week 20) | Day 141 | ± 5 days |
| V6 (End of Study / Follow-Up) | Day 169 | ± 7 days |

If a visit falls outside the allowed window, the investigator must document
the deviation in the study records and notify the sponsor's medical monitor
within 5 business days.

The follow-up visit (V6) window is ± 7 days from Day 169. This applies to
the end-of-study and safety follow-up visit.

### 3.5 Concomitant Medication

The following classes of medications are permitted during the study, subject
to the conditions described below.

**Permitted:**

- Metformin: Permitted if the subject was on a stable dose of at least
  1000 mg/day for at least 8 weeks prior to screening. The dose must remain
  stable throughout the study unless dose reduction is required for safety
  reasons, in which case the CRC must notify the monitor.
- Antihypertensive agents: Permitted if dose is stable for at least 4 weeks
  prior to screening.
- Stable lipid-lowering therapy (statins, fibrates): Permitted if dose is
  stable for at least 4 weeks prior to screening.
- Thyroid hormone replacement at stable dose: Permitted.
- Analgesics (acetaminophen/paracetamol) for occasional use: Permitted.

All concomitant medications must be recorded in the eCRF at each visit,
including name, dose, frequency, indication, and start/stop dates.

---

### 3.6 Prohibited Medication

The following antidiabetic and other medications are prohibited during the
study period:

**Prohibited antidiabetic agents:**

- GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., liraglutide, semaglutide, exenatide,
  dulaglutide, tirzepatide)
- SGLT2 inhibitors (e.g., empagliflozin, dapagliflozin, canagliflozin,
  ertugliflozin)
- Sulfonylureas (e.g., glibenclamide, glimepiride, glipizide, gliclazide)
- Thiazolidinediones (e.g., pioglitazone, rosiglitazone)
- DPP-4 inhibitors (e.g., sitagliptin, alogliptin, saxagliptin, linagliptin)
- Insulin (any formulation), except short-term rescue therapy as specified in
  Section 4 (Exceptions)
- Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors (e.g., acarbose, miglitol)
- Meglitinides (e.g., repaglinide, nateglinide)
- Any other investigational antidiabetic agent

**Other prohibited agents:**

- Systemic glucocorticoids (except short courses <= 7 days for acute illness;
  must be documented and notified to the monitor)
- Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors or inducers (unless approved by Medical Monitor)

If a subject inadvertently takes a prohibited medication, the event must be
documented as a protocol deviation and reported to the sponsor within 5
business days.

### 3.7 Safety Reporting — Adverse Events and SAEs

#### 3.7.1 Adverse Events (AEs)

All AEs observed or reported by the subject during the study period must be
documented in the source document and eCRF at each visit, including:
- Description, severity (mild/moderate/severe), and outcome
- Relationship to study treatment (related / possibly related / unrelated)
- Action taken (none / dose modification / medication added / withdrawal)

#### 3.7.2 Serious Adverse Events (SAEs)

An SAE is any adverse event that:
- Results in death
- Is life-threatening
- Requires inpatient hospitalization or prolongation of existing hospitalization
- Results in persistent or significant disability or incapacity
- Is a congenital anomaly or birth defect
- Is considered medically important by the Investigator

**Reporting timeline:**

All SAEs must be reported to the sponsor's pharmacovigilance department
within 24 hours of the site becoming aware of the event, regardless of
relationship to study treatment.

The initial SAE report may be submitted by telephone, followed by a written
report using the SAE Report Form within 5 calendar days.

If additional information becomes available after the initial report, a
follow-up SAE report must be submitted within 5 calendar days of receiving
the new information.

Fatal or life-threatening SAEs must be reported to the relevant regulatory
authority (ANMAT, ANVISA, or other applicable authority) per local regulatory
requirements, typically within 7 calendar days of the site becoming aware.

#### 3.7.3 SAE Follow-Up

SAEs must be followed until resolution, return to baseline, or determination
of a stable, final outcome. Final SAE reports must include outcome and
causality assessment.

### 3.8 Missed Dose Management

If a subject misses a dose of IMT-201:

- **Single missed dose:** If the subject remembers within 12 hours of the
  scheduled dose time, the dose should be taken as soon as remembered. If
  more than 12 hours have elapsed, the subject should skip the missed dose
  and continue with the next scheduled dose.
- **Dose must not be doubled.** Under no circumstances should a subject take
  two doses to compensate for one missed dose.
- **Two or more consecutive missed doses:** The investigator must be notified
  within 24 hours. The investigator will assess whether the subject can
  continue in the study and document the decision in the source record and
  eCRF.
- **Compliance threshold:** Subjects who miss more than 20% of doses over
  any 4-week period may be considered for discontinuation at the
  investigator's discretion.

All missed doses must be recorded in the subject diary and reconciled at each
visit during the compliance check.

See also Pharmacy Manual Section 3.4 for dispensation and accountability
procedures related to missed doses.

## 4. Exceptions

### 4.1 Rescue Therapy

If a subject's fasting plasma glucose exceeds 300 mg/dL on two consecutive
measurements, or if the subject develops symptomatic hyperglycemia requiring
immediate treatment, rescue therapy with short-acting insulin may be initiated
at the investigator's discretion after notifying the sponsor's Medical Monitor.
Subjects requiring rescue insulin that extends beyond 14 days may be
discontinued from the study.

### 4.2 Visit Window Violations

If a subject cannot attend a visit within the allowed window, the investigator
must:
1. Document the reason and the actual visit date in the source record and eCRF
2. Report the deviation to the sponsor within 5 business days
3. Ensure that all planned assessments are completed at the next available visit
   if clinically appropriate

### 4.3 Rescreening

A subject who fails screening may be rescreened once within 60 days of the
initial screening date, at the discretion of the Investigator and with
notification to the sponsor. The reason for rescreening must be documented.

### 4.4 Protocol Deviations

Any deviation from this protocol must be documented and reported according to
the study procedures described in the Study Procedures Manual.
