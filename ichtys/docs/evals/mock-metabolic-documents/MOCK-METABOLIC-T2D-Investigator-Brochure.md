## 1. Purpose

This Investigator Brochure (IB) provides clinical investigators with a summary
of the available preclinical and clinical information on IMT-201, a fictional
investigational oral agent under evaluation for the treatment of type 2 diabetes
mellitus (T2DM) in adults with overweight or obesity.

The purpose of this IB is to support investigators in understanding:
- The pharmacological and safety profile of IMT-201
- The known and potential risks associated with its administration
- The rationale for the study design and dose selection used in
  MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1

This document does not represent any real investigational product, clinical
study, or sponsor. It contains no real patient data, regulatory submissions,
or proprietary pharmaceutical information.

This IB must be read in conjunction with the study protocol. In the event of
any discrepancy, the protocol takes precedence unless otherwise instructed by

## 3. Procedures / Criteria

### 3.1 Product Overview

**Name:** IMT-201 (fictional)
**Class:** Selective partial agonist of the GPR-X receptor (fictional mechanism)
**Formulation (mock):** Oral tablet, 25 mg and 50 mg strengths
**Route of administration:** Oral, once daily with or without food
**Mechanism of action (fictional):** IMT-201 is a selective partial agonist at
the GPR-X receptor, a G-protein-coupled receptor expressed in pancreatic beta
cells and adipose tissue. In preclinical models, GPR-X activation increased
glucose-stimulated insulin secretion, reduced hepatic glucose output, and
modestly improved insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, without inducing
significant weight-independent hypoglycemia.

IMT-201 does not belong to any existing approved antidiabetic drug class.
Its mechanism is distinct from GLP-1 receptor agonists, SGLT2 inhibitors,
sulfonylureas, DPP-4 inhibitors, and thiazolidinediones.

**Pharmacokinetics (mock):** IMT-201 reaches peak plasma concentration (Tmax)
approximately 2 to 3 hours after oral administration. Bioavailability is
estimated at 68% in healthy volunteers. Half-life (t1/2) is approximately
18 hours, supporting once-daily dosing. IMT-201 is metabolized primarily by
CYP3A4; concomitant use of strong CYP3A4 inhibitors or inducers requires
medical monitor approval (see Protocol Section 3.6).

### 3.2 Summary of Known and Potential Risks

The following safety signals have been identified in nonclinical studies and
early-phase clinical trials of IMT-201 with mock data. This information is
provided to guide monitoring and safety reporting.

**Known risks:**

1. **Gastrointestinal symptoms:** Nausea, abdominal discomfort, and loose
   stools have been reported in approximately 15–20% of subjects in Phase 1
   studies. These events were generally mild to moderate, transient, and
   resolved without treatment discontinuation in most cases.

2. **Hypoglycemia risk with sulfonylureas or insulin:** In combination with
   secretagogues such as sulfonylureas or insulin, there is a theoretical
   additive risk of hypoglycemia. Because these medications are prohibited in
   MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 (see Protocol Section 3.6), this risk is mitigated
   by design. Subjects on stable metformin have not shown increased
   hypoglycemia risk in mock nonclinical models.

3. **Pancreatic safety monitoring:** Given the mechanism of action involving
   pancreatic beta cells, pancreatic enzyme levels (amylase, lipase) are
   monitored at selected visits. Any clinical or biochemical signs of
   pancreatitis must be reported immediately (see Protocol Section 3.7).
   Subjects with a history of pancreatitis are excluded from enrollment
   (see Protocol Section 3.2).

**Potential risks (to be characterized):**

4. **Cardiac safety:** QT prolongation has not been identified in available
   mock preclinical studies, but cardiac monitoring (ECG at V1 and V6) is
   included as a precautionary measure.

5. **Hepatic effects:** Mild transient increases in ALT/AST were observed in
   <5% of subjects in Phase 1. Subjects with significant hepatic impairment
   are excluded from enrollment (see Protocol Section 3.2).

### 3.3 Concomitant Medication Considerations

Based on the pharmacokinetic profile and mechanism of IMT-201:

- **Metformin:** The combination of IMT-201 with metformin was evaluated in
  mock nonclinical studies. No pharmacokinetic interaction was identified.
  Metformin at stable dose is permitted in MOCK-METABOLIC-T2D-v1 per Protocol
  Section 3.5, provided the dose has been stable for at least 8 weeks prior
  to screening.
- **GLP-1 receptor agonists:** Not evaluated in combination with IMT-201.
  GLP-1 receptor agonists are prohibited per Protocol Section 3.6 to avoid
  confounding efficacy signals.
- **Insulin:** Prohibited except for rescue use per Protocol Section 4.1.
  There is a theoretical hypoglycemia risk if combined with IMT-201.
- **CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers:** Given that IMT-201 is metabolized by
  CYP3A4, concomitant use of strong inhibitors (e.g., ketoconazole,
  clarithromycin) or strong inducers (e.g., rifampin) should be avoided
  unless approved by the Medical Monitor.

In all cases, the protocol definitions of permitted and prohibited medications
(Sections 3.5 and 3.6) take precedence over this IB. If there is any conflict
between the IB and the protocol on concomitant medication, the investigator
must contact the Medical Monitor before proceeding.

### 3.4 Safety Monitoring Considerations

Investigators are expected to:
1. Record all adverse events at each study visit, regardless of relationship
   to IMT-201.
2. Report SAEs to the sponsor within 24 hours of becoming aware, per Protocol
   Section 3.7.
3. Monitor for gastrointestinal symptoms, especially during the first 4 weeks
   of treatment.
4. Monitor for signs and symptoms of pancreatitis (abdominal pain, elevated
   lipase/amylase) throughout the study period.
5. Ensure ECG is performed at V1 and V6.
6. Document all protocol deviations related to safety management in the source
   record and eCRF.
